Shortlist

Name styles (multi-select) decide what gets generated: Real words (single dictionary words), Two words, Brandable (invented, pronounceable), Misspelled (Flickr / Lyte-style respellings), Prefix and Suffix patterns, Blend (portmanteaus), Alliterative pairs, Short & punchy (4–5 letters), Classic roots (Latin / Greek), and Nature & animals. Tick any combination; the pool is built from all of them.

The search scores every name, then checks domains best-first against public DNS until it fills How many to find in the states ticked under Find — or runs out. Short real-word .coms are mostly registered, so for lots of open names lean on Brandable / Short / Misspelled, add .io or .ai, or allow longer names.

The value score weighs length, ending, word quality, pronounceability, cleanliness, and a trend boost (AI, crypto, health, energy, lending). It estimates appeal, not a guaranteed price. States: open means unregistered — register it today at any registrar (confirmed with a second DNS lookup so it's trustworthy); for sale means parked at a marketplace, buyable at the owner's price; taken means registered and not listed; unverified means DNS was ambiguous (grouped with taken, off by default). Open and For sale are the ones you can buy. Confirm on Namecheap before paying.

More buying options. Open names link out to several registrars — Namecheap, Porkbun, Dynadot, GoDaddy — so you can cross-check availability and compare registration prices, since a name not showing on one sometimes shows on another. For-sale names get a tile listing the marketplaces they may be sold on (Sedo, Afternic, Dan, GoDaddy, Atom) in one place. Live asking prices can't be read in the browser (marketplaces block cross-site requests and there's no free price feed), so the tile links out to each listing; pulling real prices and merging duplicate listings needs those marketplaces' APIs through a backend.
